Before Angular 21, we had two different types of forms in Angular: Template-driven forms and Reactive forms. Both of these approaches had their own advantages and disadvantages, and developers had to choose between them based on their specific use case.

With the introduction of Angular 21, a new type of form called "Signal Forms" has been introduced. Now, right now, they are experimental and not yet recommended for production use, but they offer a new way to handle forms in Angular.

As the name suggests, they use 'signals' to manage form state and interactions. This approach allows for a more reactive way of handling forms, making it easier to manage complex form logic and state changes.

In this section, we will learn how we can set up and use Signal Forms in Angular. We will cover the basics of creating a Signal Form, handling form controls, and managing form state.
